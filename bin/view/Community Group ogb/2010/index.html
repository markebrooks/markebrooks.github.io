Details

  1. Eligibility to Vote and Polling Credentials
    1. You are entitled to vote in the Elections if you had a valid Core Contributor Grant as of Feb 14, 2010.  Check your status at https://auth.opensolaris.org/collectives.action  If this info is incorrect, please report this to your group leaders immediately and file a bug in http://defect.opensolaris.org under the Community/grants category.
    2. Detailed instructions can be found at the poll web site.
  2. OGB Nominations
    1. Any OpenSolaris community member can be nominated to stand for election to the OGB - all you need is a valid log-in ID for OpenSolaris.org. Nominations must however be made by a Core Contributor and accepted by the candidate.
    2. The final deadline for all nominations and acceptances to be received and recorded in the mail archive at ogb-discuss@opensolaris.org is 23:59 US/Pacific on Monday, March 1, 2010.
    3. Full details are in Valerie's e-mail 
    4. A list of nominations appears below.
  3. New Constitution
    1. The OGB has noted all the comments made publicly and privately and has now published the final proposal for the new Constitution.
    2. We believe this new document simplifies and clarifies the community governance to match our actual practice and realistic expectations.
    3. The current OGB is unanimously in favor of the new constitution and recommends a 'Yes' vote.
  4. Meeting of Members
    1. Under the terms of the existing Constitution, we have to gather a quorum of the voting membership on irc://irc.freenode.net/#opensolaris-meeting starting at noon US/Pacific on Thursday, February 25th.  Quorum has been achieved.
    2. Please log in to the IRC channel and announce yourself in the format "ogbot register <your OS.o ID> as <your name>" (without the quotes, duh!)
    3. There is a bot monitoring the channel and recording these announcements as well as all channel discussion (logs)
    4. All comments made after the start of the meeting will be recorded by the bot as minutes of the meeting.
    5. Feel free to use the IRC channel to ask, or indeed answer, any questions about the election. The current OGB can be identified as channel operators on the IRC channel.
