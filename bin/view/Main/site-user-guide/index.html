Site User Guide

This document outlines how to use opensolaris.org. The page is organized into tasks that users would perform in various circumstances based on their role. All users can see how their roles fit into the overall structure of the community by reading the Roles & Collectives document. Also, throughout the document there are pointers to other resources to help users operate more effectively. The OpenSolaris Website Engineering Team maintains this document.

  • All users are expected to be on the opensolaris-announce mailing list for update announcements about the website. Subscribe here
  • For comments or help, send mail to  website-admin AT opensolaris DOT org.

General Information

Agreements, Roles, Collectives

Agreements are required for community members and for Oracle employees before they can obtain the role of Leader or Developer in Projects and use the core development infrastructure on the site. However, agreements are not required for the role of Participant in Projects, and agreements are not required for any role in Community Groups and User Groups. For more detail see the Roles & Collectives document.

Managing Collective Relationships

Users can manage their collective relationships in the website's Auth application.

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Choose 'Collectives' from the menu on the left side of the page.
  4. On the next page, the top box enables all users to view and delete relationships they have with their own collectives and add themselves as a Participate to all collectives. If the user has a Leader role, there will be another box at the bottom of the page listing collectives the Leader can manage.

Obtaining a Role in a Collective

How roles are obtained varies depending on the collective and the role. Anyone registered on the site can self-assign the Participant role using the Auth application, and this role can also be granted by a collective Leader. Other roles, however, are granted only by collective Leaders, and decisions are based on merit. Users interested in obtaining a role beyond Participant should do the following:

  1. Subscribe to a collective's mailing list(s).
  2. Actively participate to get to know the people and the issues in the collective.
  3. Determine in collaboration with others how you can best contribute and obtain the desired role.

More information about contributing throughout the OpenSolaris community can be found on the participation page.

Creating new Collectives

The processes for creating new collectives -- Community Groups, Projects, User Groups -- on the website are outlined in detail on the Infrastructure Life Cycle Instructions page. Although the processes differ slightly for each collective, they all share these common characteristics:

  1. Proposals, open discussion, and voting on list.
  2. Setup interactions with the website team.
  3. Life cycle maintenance.

Ask questions about creating new collectives by posting mail to website-admin AT opensolaris DOT org.

Administrative Actions for Collective Leaders

Leaders can view, add, modify, and delete other users in collectives.

Viewing Existing Users in a Collective

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Choose 'Collectives' from the menu on the left side of the page.
  4. On the next page, scroll down to the box at the bottom of the page listing collectives that can be managed.
  5. Click Edit (blue pencil icon) under the Action column for any collective to view a list of users.

Searching for and Adding Users to a Collective

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Choose 'Collectives' from the menu on the left side of the page.
  4. On the next page, scroll down to the box at the bottom of the page listing collectives that can be managed.
  5. Click 'Edit' (blue pencil icon) under the Action column for any collective to view a list of users.
  6. Click the 'Add a user relationship' link to search for a user.
  7. Search for the user via User Name, Real Name, Email, or Don't Care.
  8. Add the users by clicking the blue '+' icon in the Action column.
  9. Set the role in the drop down menu and click 'Save' via the blue icon in the Action column. The system will set the Start Date automatically.

Modifying User Roles in a Collective

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Choose 'Collectives' from the menu on the left side of the page.
  4. On the next page, scroll to the box at the bottom of the page listing collectives that can be managed.
  5. Click 'Edit' (blue pencil icon) under the Action column for any collective to view a list of users.
  6. Find the user in the list (click on the green arrow icon to advance to more pages if necessary).
  7. Click 'Edit' (blue pencil icon) under the Action column for any user to edit the record.

Deleting Users from a Collective

  1. Follow the steps above to Modify a user.
  2. Set an appropriate End Date in the user's record to delete the user.

User Account Management

Getting an opensolaris.org Account

  1. Register on the website.
  2. Fields marked in bold are required.
  3. The system will respond with an email validation to complete the process. The validation token is good for 24 hours only.

Managing User Accounts

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Manage Account Details, SSH Keys, Collectives, and Agreements.

Viewing Agreements

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Choose Agreements to view your agreements on file.
  4. Community members who want to contribute to the OpenSolaris project must file an Oracle Contributor Agreement (agreement here, FAQ here). Once the agreement is filed, the data will appear under the Agreements link in the user account and the user will have access to various resources.

Managing Passwords

  1. To change a password, log in to the website, choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page, and change the password in the Account Details screen.
  2. To reset a forgotten password, go to the auth application and click on the 'Forgotten your password? Reset it.' link to initiate the reset process. The system will respond to your prompt with a rest token asking for the account's security answers. The reset token is good for 24 hours only.

Getting an opensolaris.org Email Address

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Look in the second box for the following reference: 'Forward email. Select if you want to forward mail sent to username@opensolaris.org to your registered email address.'
  4. Check the box and Save your account.

Inactivating an Account

  1. Log in to the website.
  2. Choose "Edit your account" from the menu on the left side of the page.
  3. Scroll down to the bottom, click on the 'Inactive Account' button, and Save.

NOTE: All users with Inactive accounts will be unable to access opensolaris.org services that are integrated with the Auth application, such as the SCM Console, project source repositories, the code review tool, the static application, the Bugzilla database, and XWiki collective spaces.

Activating an Inactivate Account

  1. Post mail to website-admin AT opensolaris DOT org.
  2. The Website Team will respond.

Reporting Problems with Contributor Agreements, CAPTCHA, or Registrations

  1. Post mail with full details to website-admin AT opensolaris DOT org.
  2. The Website Team will respond.

Source Code Repository Management

Granting Users Access Commit Privileges to Source Code Repositories

Prerequisite: Be a Leader for the project for which you want to grant SCM privileges. Note that Leaders in a given project automatically have commit privileges to all repositories associated with the project, but Developers have to be given commit privileges on a per-repository basis via the SCM Console. See "Adding Users to a Collective" above for adding Leaders and Developers to projects. Then, to grant Developers commit privileges, follow these steps:

  1. Log in and go to the SCM application.
  2. Select "Manage your repositories" from the list of available actions.
  3. Select the Project for which you wish to grant SCM privileges.
  4. Select the Users link in the left nav.
  5. Select the Developer.
  6. Check the box for each repository to grant commit access to the Developer.

Creating a Source Code Repository

Prerequisite: Be a Leader for the project for which you want to create a source code repository.

  1. Go to the SCM application.
  2. Select "Manage your repositories" from the list of available actions.
  3. Select the Project for which you wish to create a source code repository.
  4. Select the 'Add Repository' button on the page.
  5. Complete the details and submit the form.

Populating a Source Code Repository

Prerequisite: Be a Leader, or a Developer with commit rights, for the project that owns the source code repository.

  1. Go to the SCM application.
  2. Select "View repository information" from the list of available actions to view a list of projects. Select the appropriate project and then the name of the repository to view details.
  3. Ensure that the source code repository is not locked.
  4. Check that the user is listed as a Committer by selecting the Committers link in the left nav.
  5. Checkout/clone the content using the relevant command - repo_url as shown on the source code repository details page:
  • svn co
  • hg clone
    Add the content to the source code repository that you have checked out locally. Commit/push the local content to the source code repository on http://repo.opensolaris.org.
  • svn commit
  • hg push

Deleting a Source Code Repository

Prerequisite: Be a Leader for the project for which you want to delete a source code repository. WARNING: This action will completely remove a repository and it can not be recovered.

  1. Go to the SCM application.
  2. Select "Manage your repositories" from the list of available actions.
  3. Select the Project for which you wish to delete a source code repository.
  4. Click on the red X in the table to delete a repository.

Getting Commit Privileges to a Source Code Repository

A person must have the role of Developer in a Project to get commit privileges to the Project's source code repositories. A Leader of the Project can assign the role of Leader or Developer in the Auth application by selecting 'Manage Participants' and adding the user name.

Code Review Management

To generate a code review:

Prerequisite: Be a Leader or Developer for the project that owns the code review.

  • Generate the review using the 'webrev' tool.
  • Zip up the review: 'zip -rp webrev.zip webrev'
  • Go to https://cr.opensolaris.org/ and log in.
  • Click on "Manage your Code Reviews" 
  • Select the appropriate project then select "Upload Review"
  • Enter a name and optional description for the review.
  • Click next to move onto the file upload.
  • Click Browse and select the review ZIP file you wish to upload.
  • Click Save.

Note that uploading may take time depending on the size of the review. The maximum upload size is 32MB.

To view a review:

Prerequisite: All community members can view reviews.

  • Go to https://cr.opensolaris.org/.
  • Select "Browse your code reviews" and select the appropriate project.

XWiki Content Management

Viewing/Selecting Page Information

  1. Navigate to a page for which you want to view/select the following page information: Comments, Attachments, History, Information, Source.
  2. Hover the mouse over the View menu and select the appropriate item. NOTE: Clicking on the View menu item will show the page source.

Commenting on a Page

Prerequisites: Have an account on opensolaris.org and be a Participant/Affiliate/Developer/Leader in the Collective that owns the page to which you want to add a comment.

  1. Navigate to the page to which you want to add a comment.
  2. Hover the mouse over the View menu item and select Comments.
  3. Enter your comment and click the Save button.

Editing a Page

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page for which you have edit privileges.

  1. Click the Edit menu item. The page will move to edit mode and default to the Wiki editor.
  2. Use the Wiki editor or choose the WYSIWYG editor in the panel on the right side of the page.
  3. Enter your content.
  4. Preview, Save & Continue, Save & View, or Cancel at the bottom of the content edit box.
  5. Editing help is at XWiki Syntax Help at the bottom of the right panel on each page in edit mode.

Creating a Page

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be in a collective in which you have edit privileges.

  1. Select Create Page from the 'OS' menu at the top of the page named for the collective you are in. For example: if you are in the Advocacy Community, hover the mouse over the 'OS Community Group Advocacy' menu and select Create Page.
  2. On the following page, the 'Space and Page Name' and Parent fields will be filled in by default, but you must complete the name of your page by adding a name after the '.' in the 'Space and Page Name' field. The text added will be the final part of the page URL.
  3. Click the Create button. The page will move to edit mode and default to the Wiki editor.
  4. Use the Wiki editor or choose the WYSIWYG editor in the panel on the right side of the page.
  5. Enter your content.
  6. Preview, Save & Continue, Save & View, or Cancel at the bottom of the content edit box.
  7. Editing help is at XWiki Syntax Help at the bottom of the right panel on each page in edit mode.

Deleting a Page from a Collective

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be in a collective for which you are a Leader.

  1. Navigate to the page you want to delete and select Delete from the Actions menu in the top navigation bar.
  2. Click the Yes button to move the page to the recycle bin.

NOTE: If the page has attachments on it, you must delete the attachments before deleting the page.  To see attachments, click on View->Attachments.

Managing Attachments

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page that has attachments and for which you have edit privileges.

  1. Select View -> Attachments from the top menu.
  2. Upload and delete attachments.

Note: If attachments with the same file name are uploaded, a new version number will appear. To see the version history, click the version number next to the file name. Also, there is a 13MB limit to attachments. Use the Static application (instructions below) for uploads of up to 32MB).

Using Attachments

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page that has attachments and for which you have edit privileges. There are two options available to link to attachments:

  1. Option 1: Link to attachments in text by using the full URL to the attachment if known. In the WYSIWYG editor, select the text to be linked, click on Link -> Web Page, and insert the web address to the attachment to create the link.
  2. Option 2: Link to attachments in text by navigating to the attachment. In the WYSIWYG editor, select Link -> Attached File, navigate to the appropriate page with the attachment, and select the attachment to create the link.

Making Attachments Display on a Page

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page for which you have edit privileges.

  1. Click the Edit button in the top navigation Menu.
  2. Choose the Wiki editor.
  3. Insert the macro below in the edit box in the desired location.
{{velocity}}
#template("listattachments.vm")
{{/velocity}}
  • Click the Preview button to display the page layout.
  • Click the Save button to save the page changes.
  • After saving, the page will display the attachments in a table.

Adding a Flash File

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page for which you have edit privileges.

  1. Create, or navigate to, the page that will contain the Flash file.
  2. Follow the Instructions from "Managing Attachments" above to attach the swf file(s) to the page.
  3. Click the Edit button in the top navigation menu.
  4. Choose your editor type as "Wiki".
  5. Enter the following velocity Macro into your page:
{{velocity}}
{{html wiki=true}}
#set($width = "640")
#set($height= "480")
#set($flashurl = $doc.getAttachmentURL("SWFexample.swf"))
#template("flash.vm")
{{/html}}
{{/velocity}}
  • Click Save & View to save your changes.
  • The values for $width and $height should be replaced with the values from the Flash file(s) attached in Step 2.
  • The file name, shown here as SWFexample.swf should be replaced with the name of the Flash file(s) attached in Step 2.

Note: You can also embed a Flash file from a different page or server by replacing the  #set($flashurl = value as follows:

#set($flashurl = "/bin/download/Community+Group+zfs/basics/zfsExample.swf")

Adding a Blog and/or RSS Feed

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and be on a page for which you have edit privileges.

  1. Click the Edit button.
  2. Select Insert Macro from the Macro menu.
  3. Select RSS feed from the list.
  4. Complete the entry fields, inputting your blog RSS in the Feed entry field, and click the Apply button.
  5. Click Save & View to save your changes. The blog feed appears on the wiki page.

Static Publication Management

For Viewing Publications

Prerequisites: Have the appropriate privileges to view files.

  1. Point your browser to static.opensolaris.org.
  2. If logged in, click on Browse your Publications in the left nav to view a list of Collectives that have content you may have privileges to view.
  3. If not logged in, click on the appropriate Collective displayed on the page to view the content you may have privileges to view.

For Managing Publications

Prerequisites: Be logged in to the website and have privileges in your Collective to manage publications (generally a Leader or Developer).

  1. Go to static.opensolaris.org.
  2. Click on Manage your Publications in the left nav.
  3. Click on the Create Publication button, enter data in the fields (no spaces in the Publication field), and click save. NOTE: If the Anonymous box is checked at the bottom of the Create Publication screen, the publication will be viewable to the public. If the Anonymous box is not checked, privileges for viewing/uploading publications can be set in the Rights section for everyone in the Collective with a specific Role or for individual Users within a Role.
  4. Click on the Content link in the left nav to upload content (32MB limit for each file). Each Publication can support multiple files, and each Collective can support multiple Publications.
  5. Click on the Rights link in the left nav to assign privileges for viewing/uploading publications by Role or by User.
