 I think that you can only vote for someone if you have a
 *feeling* for their ability to perform the job; for example
 through following their comments etc. in forums like c.u.s
 and through their blogs (when blogging truly takes off). Our
  current nominees are (apparently) Rich Teer and Jörg
 Schilling have been influential/active for yonks, and Ben
 Rockwood has just stormed in as a top player. Selecting your
 nominee(s) based on a few short paragraphs (eg. snapped from
 their CV ;-) ) will not help you make an informed selection.
 > The OpenSolaris Web Site is open, and is openly advertising the selection of
 > the CAB and the structural makeup of it for the first 12 months. I see no
 > obstacle to opening this process to the public. Besides I'm not proposing a
 > campaign speech. Just a description of the background and disclosure of the

 > information. If you let views become part of the "Profile" then it's too easy

 A publicly-accessible subset of the The OpenSolaris website
 is open. It also states that the CAB will be: "composed of 5
 members (2 appointed by Sun, 2 elected by the pilot
 community and 1 selected by Sun from the broader open source
 community)". Note "2 elected by the pilot community". *Not*
 by the public. Currently *we* are the budding community: the
 public is just being given a taster of OpenSolaris, and the
 assurance that there *is* already a community, which there
 undoubtedly is. Another quote: "The OpenSolaris CAB is just
 the first step, but it is an important step. The Pilot
 community is ready to begin their nominations and Sun is
 considering its best candidates for the Sun-appointed seats.
 The names of the OpenSolaris CAB members will be posted on
 this page in March 2005 and we will then start an interest
 mail list for those who want to follow the workings of the
 OpenSolaris CAB.". Currently it is us and Sun who will
 choose the CAB members - the public is not invited.
 > Also, bear in mind that the candidates selected now are going to preside over
 > the initial delivery of the OpenSolaris project and will bear bear the full
 > force of initial media interest. They will be almost certainly subjected to
 > media scrutiny after the fact. It's important for me (at least) to know that
 > the community delegates can handle this well.
 "Preside over" is too strong. "Steer" is closer. And "bear
 the full force of initial media interest" is probably an
 overstatement; as I am certain that the CAB members selected
 by our community will still be under NDA, and I would not be
 surprised that a specific one-liner media-related NDA ("I
 will not under any circumstances talk to the press. Sign
 here.....") might also be required.
 Sean/Beardy.
